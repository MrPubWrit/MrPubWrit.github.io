User Manual: "The Job-Seeker's Guide to Microsoft Word"

I learned how to create modular content, include descriptive instructions and effectively integrate technical screenshots and callouts into text.

"AI and you" (Massachusetts Daily Collegian article)

I wrote the article "AI and you" for the Massachusetts Daily Collegian on December 9, 2025. It is an examination of how students at the University of Massachusetts use AI for various tasks, including education, how they feel about the potential ramifications of AI on their proposed careers and what ethical frameworks people can use AI within. The goal of this article was to introduce the topic of AI and relate it in multiple ways to a general audience.

"Randolph W. Bromery at 100: how his Chancellorship shaped UMass" (Massachusetts Daily Collegian article)

I wrote the article "Randolph W. Bromery at 100: how his Chancellorship shaped UMass" for the Massachusetts Daily Collegian on February 26, 2026. It explores the life of Randolph W. Bromery, the second Chancellor (and first Black Chancellor) of the University of Massachusetts, and his contributions to the University over his eight-year tenure as Chancellor. With Bromery having served as Chancellor from 1971 to 1979, the article's main purpose was to explain the significance of Bromery's long-term initiatives and policies to the University at present.

View "Randolph W. Bromery at 100: how his Chancellorship shaped UMass" (Massachusetts Daily Collegian website)

"Elvis Presley at 90: who was the musician and who made him?" (Massachusetts Daily Collegian article)

I wrote the article "Elvis Presley at 90: who was the musician and who made him" for the Massachusetts Daily Collegian on January 8, 2025. It focuses on the long-term cultural impacts of Elvis Presley, including the Black musical influences he gained inspiration from and the debate over whether he appropriated the music of the Black communities or not. The objective of the article was to explain the long-term cultural impacts of Presley's work, which occurred in the 1950s, 1960s and 1970s.
