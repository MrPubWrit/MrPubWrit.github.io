German luxury cars

German vehicle companies are well-known for building luxury cars, which have proven quite popular with people in various parts of the world. We will be focusing on four such brands in this page:

  1. Mercedes-Benz
  2. BMW
  3. Audi
  4. Porsche

Mercedes-Benz

Mercedes-Benz as we know it was founded in 1926 - nearly 100 years ago - by Karl Benz, Gottlieb Daimler, Wilhelm Maybach and Emil Jellinek. It was 40 years after Karl Benz became the first person to file a patent for the modern automobile. Among Mercedes-Benz's most notable models are:

  • A-Class (subcompact executive car)
  • C-Class (compact executive car)
  • E-Class (mid-size executive car)
  • G-Class (full-size executive SUV)
  • S-Class (full-size executive car)

Audi

Audi as we know it was founded in 1949, though several initial versions of the company were made before that. Along with brands including Porsche, Skoda and SEAT, Audi is a subsidiary of Volkswagen Group, and many of Audi's cars share engines and chassis with Volkswagen's cars. Among Audi's most notable models are:

  • A4 (compact executive car)
  • A6 (mid-size executive car)
  • A8 (full-size executive car)
  • Q5 (mid-size executive SUV)
  • Q7 (full-size executive SUV)

BMW

BMW (short for "Bayerische Motoren Werke") was founded in 1916; it started out as a manufacturer for planes during the First World War. It then moved on to making motorbikes and then cars. Among BMW's most notable models are:

  • 3 Series (compact executive car)
  • 5 Series (mid-size executive car)
  • M5 (mid-size executive sports car; a sportier version of the 5 Series)
  • M7 (full-size executive car)
  • X5 (mid-size executive SUV)

Porsche

In 1931, Ferdinand Porsche founded the Porsche brand. It was initially an automobile consulting company, offering designs but not manufacturing until 1948. Its first car, launched that year, was the 356. Although best known for its sports cars, including the 911, it has also manufactured touring cars and SUVs. Among Porsche's most notable models are:

  • 911 (sports car)
  • Boxster (sports car)
  • Macan (compact executive SUV)
  • Cayenne (mid-size executive SUV)
  • Panamera (full-size executive car)
