ClipChamp and audio description

Adding audio description to an existing video

In order to be accessible to anyone, adding an audio description to a video you have created can be very helpful. Someone with vision impairment may not be able to see everything happening in the video, so adding an audio description will ensure that they will be able to interact with your content with the same ease as anyone else.

  1. Click upload a video on the home page of the ClipChamp website. Your video files open.
  2. Choose the video that you would like to add an audio description to. A new tab opens with your video.
  3. Click Open Editor at the ribbon on the top of the page. A popup appears.
  4. Click the purple Copy Now button at the bottom of the Edit in ClipChamp Editor popup. A new tab opens in Editor.
    Note: If you have checked the Don’t show this again button previously, ClipChamp will not show the popup and instead will go directly to the editing software. In this case, you can disregard step 4 and go right to step 5.
  5. In the sidebar, click the Record and create button. Different recording options appear.
  6. Click Audio. A recording feature opens in the window.
  7. Click the Start recording button. You can now record whatever audio that you would like to put over your existing video.
  8. Once you are done recording, click Stop & review. You can now retake the recording or continue by clicking Save and edit.
  9. You can now adjust the recording, start or end the audio exactly where you please, or shorten it.

Recording a tutorial or demo using integrated description

When recording content, you should be sure to use integrated description to keep your content clear for everyone. In order to do this, you can use the teleprompter tool built into ClipChamp.

  1. Click Screen recording on the ClipChamp homepage. A new recording window opens up in your browser.
  2. Click the Script button below the recording screen. A teleprompter opens up and you can now type what you would like to say during the tutorial, including integrated description. It stays open during the recording
    .

  3. When you have finished typing up your script, click Start Screen Recording. A popup appears for you to choose which tab you want to record.

  4. Click Share and the tab you chose immediately opens and starts the recording.

  5. Click back to the ClipChamp tab to read from the teleprompter.

  6. When you have finished with your recording, click the Stop and review button. You now have the ability to record again, pick up where you left off, or review the video.
