Genre Analysis of Massachusetts Daily Collegian Articles

View the Genre Analysis (PDF)

I wrote this genre analysis essay for my Introduction to Writing, Rhetoric and Literacy Studies class (English 250) examining news articles from the Massachusetts Daily Collegian and how they used structure, language, purpose and typography to resonate with readers. Through this assignment, I learned to discuss how newspapers could resonate with their communities and beyond through writing.

Research Essay on the Rhetorical Effects of Reddit

View the Research Essay (PDF)

I wrote this research essay for my Studies in Textuality and New Media class (English 302) discussing how Reddit, more specifically the r/UMass subreddit, could use writing as a way to connect people and spread information informally. Through this assignment, I learned to discuss how writing could be used as a tool of communication not simply from one entity to another but also among people as a group.

Research Essay on No-No Boy and In The Name Of The Father

View the Research Essay

For my Modern Novel class (English 362), I wrote this essay on the similarity of the characters and experiences of Ichiro Yamada in John Okada's No-No Boy and Gerry Conlon's character from the biographical film In The Name Of The Father. Through this assignment, I learned how to effectively use literature to draw inferences from real-life events, which is a key function of language in general.

Literary Analysis of Yellowface and the Publishing World

View the Literary Analysis (PDF)

For my Asian American literature class (English 204), I wrote this essay on how R.F. Kuang's novel Yellowface examined the publishing world and its competitiveness, racial barriers and stereotyping through satire. Through this assignment, I learned how to analyze various types of literature, including less "serious" literature in tone, and how they discussed real-life societal issues in varied ways.
